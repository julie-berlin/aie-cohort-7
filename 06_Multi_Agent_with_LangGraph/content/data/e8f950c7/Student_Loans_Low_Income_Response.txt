Student loans are often a critical resource for low-income students seeking to further their education. They can help bridge the gap between the cost of attendance and what families can afford. Here are a few key points to consider:

1. **Types of Student Loans:**  
   There are federal and private student loans available. Federal loans usually offer lower interest rates and better repayment conditions. Among them, Direct Subsidized Loans are particularly beneficial for low-income students, as they do not accrue interest while the student is enrolled in school.

2. **Income-Driven Repayment Plans:**  
   For federal loans, there are income-driven repayment plans that adjust your payments based on your earnings, which can be very helpful for low-income borrowers.

3. **Loan Forgiveness Programs:**  
   Some borrowers may qualify for loan forgiveness programs, especially if they work in public service or certain teaching fields. These programs can significantly reduce the financial burden after graduation.
